Elemental Attunement: Shadow

Like the Light element, the Shadow element has two major components. While Light-elements can generate light, Shadow-elements can absorb it. While Lightelements can order light, Shadow-elements can disorder it. Practically speaking, these two abilities are not as distinct as Light’s two abilities, though they both do have their uses. Shadow-elements have control over a similar range of the frequency spectrum, though they tend to skew a bit towards the lower frequencies. That means that while Lightelements have a harder time producing red than violet, Shadow-elements have a harder time absorbing violet than red. Their controllable frequency range is also slightly larger, and it’s virtually unheard of for a Shadow-element to be unable to interact with visible violet light in the same way some Light-elements can’t interact with red light. They can absorb about the same amount of light a Light-element can produce, though again, their capacity to absorb light is generally a little bit higher than a Lightelement’s capacity to emit it. They can also cause light to scatter, which can be used for defocusing lights and blurring vision. The Shadow element is less tricky than the Light element is – Shadowelements cannot create illusions, and do not have any natural way to weaponize 47 darkness the way a Light-element can weaponize light as a laser or blinding flash. Basically, the Light element can cause people to see things that aren’t there, and the Shadow element can cause people to not see things that are there. They can create zones of near-absolute darkness even under very bright conditions, and can impede or frustrate somebody’s ability to see by scattering pre-existing light. Combining these two applications allows Shadow-elements to become almost entirely invisible in dim lighting, as they can darken themselves and the space around them to avoid standing out, and then blur the area to disguise the fact that the darkness is unnatural – while a Shadow-element operating in daylight will always be quite conspicuous (as using absorption powers on photons before they hit a solid surface will cause people to see a very strange-looking space of absolute darkness), during the night they can make sure that they’re always just out of sight. This is as close to “true” invisibility as any element can get – while a Light-element can hide themselves in daylight by bending light around them, they require a very rigid amount of space to do this, and somebody near them, or an object passing into the controlled light will render them visible. Shadow-elements can go unseen through almost any darkness, anywhere. On top of this, a Shadow-element’s zone of intuitive control does not require a line of sight, meaning that it is essentially impossible to surprise them in low-light conditions. Shadow-elements are quiet, intuitive introverts who are quite good at figuring out when something is wrong with somebody. Like Light-elements, they shun leadership roles in favour of supporting those around them, but they instead of motivating people they often prefer to comfort them. They firmly believe that humans are inclined to periods of necessary inaction, and would rather help people affected by a problem than fix the problem itself. They are often extremely kind, well-meaning, and giving people, but if they aren’t careful they can forget the value of fixing problems and become resigned to their perpetual existence, making them gloomy and fatalistic. They can be so conflict-averse that they will quietly endure easily fixable problems, rather than tell somebody what the problem is. Unsurprisingly, they manifest healing and empathic auxiliary powers more than any other element. 6% of elementalists are Shadow-elements, as is the case with Light.
