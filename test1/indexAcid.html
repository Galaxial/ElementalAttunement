Elemental Attunement: Acid

The Acid element is something of a misnomer – it can control substances with both very high and very low pH values with equal ease/discomfort. It is predisposed to controlling substances that are liquid at median temperature, though with enough skill an Acidelement can also control solid acids. Unlike other coarse manipulation elements, Acid-elements do not target a particular chemicals – while an Acidelement cannot control water normally, a water-based solution with a very high/low pH could theoretically be controlled. Acid-elements typically control substances with pH values above 12.5 or below 1.5. Stronger Acid-elements can control substances with more neutral pH values if they exert more energy, though nobody has ever demonstrated the ability to control substances with pH values between 3 and 11. Since substances with extreme pH values are not found very commonly in nature, Acid-elements typically have to carry chemical containers with them. Furthermore, since Acid-elements have an easier time controlling substances the closer to the extremes of the pH scale it gets, they are often limited to being able to manipulate a handful of extremely destructive chemicals. This has earned the element a reputation as having no real nonviolent applications – though some chemists do find it useful around labs, there are probably less than 500 people employed this way out of a total world population of somewhere around 12 million Acid-elements. The only well-established ability Acid-elements can demonstrate beyond levitating, concentrating, or diluting extreme-pH chemicals is prevent reactions that would otherwise occur when they come in contact with things like skin, for example. This means that they can, with a bit of training, stick their hand in a bucket of concentrated battery acid and come out with nary an itch. They can control quite a lot of acid at once as well – probably more acid than you could ever reasonably be expected to have in one place. Acid-elements are typically introverts prone to strong bursts of emotion, who prioritize quick, decisive action and can’t abide laziness or anything they might perceive as a noncommittal attitude. At their best, they can be hardworking, introspective people, often possessing a quiet kind of charisma. At their worst, they can be vindictive, judgemental, and moody, prone to taking offense at things without explaining what it was that offended them. Acid-elements tend to manifest 30 auxiliary powers that deal with movement in some way, such as short-range teleportation. It is an uncommon element, accounting for about 3% of elementalists
