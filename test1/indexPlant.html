Elemental Attunement: Plant

The Plant element, as you might expect, controls mostly plants – though the specifics of what it can control are a bit broader. Plant-elements are well-known to be able to control fungi as well, but strictly speaking, it can control any living organic matter that is not linked to a brain. This means that a Plant-element could theoretically control not just plants and fungi, but also animals like jellyfish, worms, or perhaps even entire hydrozoan colonies. That having been said, all living things have some measure of IWC – it’s just that for plants, IWC is so weak it really doesn’t matter. Animals – even the ones without brains – do have enough IWC to make controlling them difficult, so most Plant-elements stick to plants and fungi. They can manipulate matter on cellular and large-scale levels, with many being able to manipulate a tree’s cell cycle one moment and then rip it out of the ground the next. Their coarse manipulation range is shorter than the other Biotic elements at about 20m, but they can exert control over about 2500kg of matter on average – more than any other element. Plant-elements can levitate, tear, bend or repair matter under their control, and also possess significant control over cellular development. This permits them to expedite or delay growth if they so desire, though they cannot 39 create matter from nowhere and consequently need to provide the nutrients that the organism would need to grow themselves. Plant-elements do lose control over matter when it dies, but they lose control by far the slowest out of all the Biotic elements, with some possessing the ability to control things that have been dead for hours or even days. Plant-elements are typically peaceful and introverted, but do love to spend time around people, provided those people come in manageable doses. They can be giving and warm, but under pressure are prone to stubbornness, with a strong inclination to resist courses of action they view as drastic even under circumstances that clearly warrant them. They are also often altruistic to the point of naiveté, and respond quite badly to people doing things they see as immoral or selfish. They don’t get angry, though – just depressed. Like Psionics-elements, they tend to develop auxiliary powers with a strongly physical angle, though these powers typically have to do with altering the state of their own body rather than empowering it to do exceptional things. Plant-elements are rare, making up 3% of all elementalists.
