Elemental Attunement:Ice

The Ice element focuses on making things cold, and controlling cold things. An Ice-element is capable of lowering the temperature of any object, and capable of exerting further control over that object once it is cold enough – though the exact conditions under which an object qualifies as “cold enough” are a bit more complicated. The most obvious restriction is that, while an Ice-element can cool off anything, they can only manipulate solids, no matter the temperature – an Iceelement can manipulate water ice at -1°C, but can’t manipulate liquid oxygen at - 182°C. Furthermore, Ice-elements have a strong predisposition to controlling substances that have a freezing point close to body temperature, and have a difficult time exerting control over solid substances with high melting points even if they are very cold. Finally, Ice-elements require exponentially greater levels of energy to cool very cold things or very hot things – which means that while freezing water is easy, they aren’t likely to force anything down to absolute zero. The point at which an Ice-element has the least difficult controlling something is when it’s at its freezing point. Combine all these restrictions, and the Ice element finds itself primarily concerned with freezing things that are liquid at median temperature and have a freezing point not far below that. An Ice-element can theoretically control any object if it’s really cold, but the chances of getting something like a metal cold enough to offset the difficulty caused by its high melting point. Ice-elements have a dual focus on the transformative aspect of their power (cooling things) and the manipulative aspect of their power (moving cold 32 things). Because the list of things that an Ice-element can reasonably control is quite short, they often work with other non-Ice-elements. They typically have a very short range (often controlling Ice at less than 10m and cooling things at less than 1m), but within that range their abilities can be quite strong. They can learn how to compromise structures by repeatedly freezing them, and can cause immense damage to technology through similar means. Ice-elements are typically shy, cautious people, and are oftentimes almost unnervingly polite. At their best, they can be kind, meticulous, and insightful, but if they don’t push themselves, they can also easily become weak-willed, fearful shut-ins. Ice is associated with a number of long-range sensory powers, and is a fairly common element, accounting for 6% of the total elementalist population
