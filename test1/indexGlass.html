Elemental Attunement: Glass

The Glass element is not misnamed, strictly speaking – it is used primarily to control glass – but strictly speaking, it controls any pure non-metallic amorphous substance. This means that it can also exert control over a large number of strange and not at all glass-like things – gels, resins, a number of different kinds of plastics, thin films, some wax… many Glass-elements go through their entire lives not knowing the full extent of their abilities – not because they contain some great, secret power, but because the exact nature of the Glass element is just so weird. Like the other coarse manipulation elements, it functions on a range of objects around median temperature – in this case, around 50°C above or below it. Unlike many others, it really doesn’t care about the state of matter that the substance is in. Glass-elements need to get creative – there’s only so far you can go by flinging broken windows at people. Some Glass-elements have demonstrated the ability to alter the state of controlled matter, allowing them to “un-break” shattered glass. As the ages have passed, Glass-elements have found themselves making increasing use of their ability to control many kinds of plastic as well. Glass-elements also typically have very, very good fine control of their element – perhaps to make up for a weak range, which often only extends to 20m. A typical Glass-element can control about 500kg of matter at once. Glass-elements are typically quiet but not withdrawn, are aesthetically minded, and have a keen appreciation of details. They can be visionary without coming across as unreasonable, and can be quite sensitive people if they turn that eye for detail towards others. At their worst, they can turn that eye for detail towards others’ faults, becoming picky, pedantic, or arrogant. Appropriately, they often manifest sensory or support powers. Glass is an uncommon element, accounting for a bit less than 3% of elementalists.
