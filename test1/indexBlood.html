Elemental Attunement: Blood

The Blood element has an intimidating name, but in practice is one of the most, if not the most, passive of the elements. Blood controls living organic matter that is linked to a brain, and, because of IWC, cannot be used on unwilling people. While Psionics-elements can detect things going on in a brain without having to exert control over it, Blood-elements are faced with a significantly less complicate, and consequently less telling, object of focus – the body. Blood-elements can detect living, sentient beings at about the same range a Psionics-element can, but can get no further information beyond a general sense of that being’s physical condition. Blood-elements exert control over cells in the same way Plantelements do, but have a drastically lowered capacity for coarse manipulation, with most Blood-elements being limited at a mere 200kg. This is not that much of an impediment, though, as Blood-elements largely care about the details of the body. While IWC prevents them from controlling others, there are many situations where somebody would let a Bloodelement control them quite willingly. They can mend injuries far better than somebody with a healing auxiliary power could, and can even reattach severed 40 limbs. They can make small modifications to existing organs as well, which, for example, allows them to cure some (but not all) kinds of blindness. They can grow bones, or reshape them painlessly, which some creative elementalists have used to make weapons from their own flesh (this is probably the only circumstance where the Blood element is as creepy as its name implies). Very, very rarely, a Blood-element learns how to repair their own DNA, which stops aging and makes them effectively immortal. If somebody is sufficiently dazed, an extremely strong Blood-element can cause physical damage to them, but even then, the target’s IWC makes causing fatal damage difficult if not impossible. Notably, while a Blood-element can repair nerves, they cannot manipulate the neural system itself, and cannot naturally repair brain injuries – though with the help of a Psionics-element this has been accomplished on rare occasions. They lose control of something within minutes of it dying, but this is long enough that they could throw around the corpse a bit if they wanted. Not many of them do. Blood-elements are typically no-nonsense pragmatists with a strong ethical code that they can often obsess over. This can make them extremely devoted and efficient humanitarians, and predisposes them to a more realistic kind of altruism than the kind that their Plant-element brethren tend towards. However, they can also come across as self-righteous and at times fanatical, and if they somehow develop an ethical code that permits a kind of ends-justify-themeans type of extremism, they can become terrifying and remorselessly cruel people. Even when this happens, they don’t lose faith in a better world – they simply don’t care if they’re the only person who thinks that the world they’re trying to create actually is better than the current one. They often develop auxiliary powers that can be used without anybody noticing. Blood is the rarest “true” element, accounting for less than 1% of elementalists.
