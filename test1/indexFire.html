Elemental Attunement: Fire

The Fire element creates and maintains non-ionized flames. Exertion of the Fire element can be used as a kind of fuel that sustains burning, and can also be used to trigger oxidization if enough heat is not present. The limits of the Fire element are well-defined but don’t often impede a Fireelement’s ability to do things. Generally, flames created by a Fire-element cannot be less than 1500°C, though, as usual, skilled elementalists can push this limit down if they want to. The main restriction of the Fire element is that it works solely on gasses undergoing oxidization, so controlling a smouldering substance like an ember is not possible. It is impossible to tell what the ideal temperature for controlling fire is, because it seems to have an upper limit that’s well above what can be reached without a state shift – the more ionized oxygen atoms there are in the flame, the harder it is for a Fireelement to control and the easier it is for a Plasma-element to control. A relatively small percentage of ionized atoms will significantly impede a fire-element’s capacity to exert control over the remaining non-ionized atoms, which means exceptionally hot flames can become impossible to control not because the state is homogenously wrong, but because there is too much interference from a nongaseous state. Fire-elements can create fires or extinguish them, and can direct preexisting flames in directions of their choosing. As is typically for Transformative Manipulation Elements, a Fire-element can direct flames at a range of up to 20m but can only create flames in spaces that are extremely close to them – oftentimes restricted to within 30cm. Fire-elements can also direct thermal energy away from themselves so long as it’s through a gaseous medium, which means that while a Fire-element can stop their own powers from burning them, if they touch a combusting substance in a non-gaseous state (i.e. an ember or superheated plasma), they will burn like a normal person. Like a Lightning-element, Fireelements need to exert elemental energy to stop themselves from burning, which 35 means if they are subject to enough fire they can also be burnt. Typically, a Fireelement will instinctively avoid exerting so much energy that they create a flame they can’t stop from burning them, but it is possible to create a flame so big or hot that they burn themselves. Fire-elements are typically focused, quiet, creative people inclined to feel strong emotions but not to show them in easily identifiable ways. This isn’t because they’re afraid of seeming vulnerable – they just process and express feelings in a different way. At their best they can be creative, driven, and emotionally intelligent people, but they can also moody or apathetic if they isolate themselves. Fire-elements manifest a very wide array of auxiliary powers, and are the most common transformative manipulation element, accounting for a bit over 8% of total elementalists.
