Empty the City

Elemental Attunement: Gravity

The Gravity element is generally regarded as one of the most powerful elements, probably because it comes the closest to true, unconditional telekinesis. It is not complicated – Gravity-elements can increase or decrease the gravity in an area, or rotate the direction in which it pulls. It considers only how far the affected area is from the elementalist, and how large that area is. Most Gravity-elements can use their powers at a distance of around 20m, and on an area about 3m3 at close range. Using the Gravity element is much more taxing than other elements, however – most elementalists cannot maintain an HEA surplus while using it in any way, which means that they will always have to stop using it sooner or later. Many Gravity-elements will prolong their use of it by burning non-elemental energy, which can leave them extremely weak if they aren’t careful. Gravity-elements can levitate themselves and others, send objects flying by rotating the direction that gravity pulls them, pin things down by increasing their weight, walk on walks and ceilings, and force others to walk on walls and ceilings with them – an experience for which few are prepared. Like the other 43 Fine Manipulation Elements, Gravity-elements can detect their element at distances further than they can actively manipulate, though for Gravity this ability is not terribly useful, as gravity on Earth tends to stay the same. They can tell when somebody else uses the Gravity element nearby, though, and they have an intuitive ability to appraise objects’ weights at a glance. Gravity-elements tend to be dynamic and outgoing, expecting lots from people and willing to work to help others meet their expectations. They are also typically fun-loving people who work hard to keep their fun far away from any sort of practical application – they are the kind people who think that if your hobby becomes your job, you’ll start to hate it. At their best they can be charismatic leaders who never succumb to self-aggrandizement, but they are also prone to setting unreasonable goals for themselves and others, and are easily disappointed. Because their elemental power puts them below HEA equilibrium so often, they typically develop weak auxiliary powers – or at least, auxiliary powers that require very little energy to use. They are uncommon, accounting for 4% of elementalists.
